The Gig Worker’s Hub: A Spotlight

Sydney · May 10, 2035

Many gig work digital platforms are encompassed in the on-demand sector of the ‘Sharing Economy’ which allows assets to be rented and shared rather than individually owned. Some have taken the idea to heart, and devised mutual aid initiatives as a way to look out for fellow gig workers.

Our focus today is the Gig Workers Hub established by Andrew Copolov in the Melbourne CBD, a shelter that provides gig workers food, drinks and access to bathrooms and charging stations. Although the original hub was closed in 2024, it has inspired the opening of similar centers in other major cities across Australia that are affiliated via a digital network.

Lori Michaels, the manager of the hub located in the Sydney CBD, tells the story of how she found out about the Gig Workers Hub.

“I actually discovered them through their website and ended up visiting a couple times. As someone who’s done deliveries to get by in the past, I thought it was a brilliant idea.

When I was there, I noticed the hub was more than just a simple rest spot, but the beginnings of an informal mutual aid network, back when unions for gig workers were less established. People who came there to get out of the rain would find themselves coming back with friends. It became a place of community and solidarity, where snacks and info were freely shared.

After it closed, I reached out to Copolov to see if it could be reopened. Over the years, with successful crowdfunding campaigns and additional grants by the city’s councils, we’ve managed to establish hubs in Sydney, Canberra, Gold Coast and Perth. The hub’s services are generously supported by online donations. It seems people really appreciate these places and just want to give back.
