ADGWU Files Landmark Case Against UStream Pty Ltd

Sydney · June 18, 2035

The Australian Digital Gig Workers Union (ADGWU) has launched a landmark class action lawsuit in the Federal Court against UStream Pty Ltd, a leading live-streaming and creator platform, alleging widespread violations of the Digital Workers Rights Act 2035.

At issue is the alleged misclassification of more than 40,000 Australian streamers and creators as “independent contractors” while subjecting them to rigid, algorithmically enforced conditions:

  • Quota-based performance metrics
  • Threats of shadowbanning
  • Automated demonetisation without explanation or appeal

“This is the first real test of the Act’s reclassification clause,” said Dr. Mathew D. Phillips, lead counsel for the ADGWU and co-author of Platform Law and Precarity in the Post-Platform Economy (2033). “Digital creators are not entrepreneurs when they’re being surveilled, scored, and penalised by algorithmic systems they have no control over.”

The case builds on prior legal and academic efforts, including the Fair Work Commission’s 2021 ruling in Deliveroo v. Franco, which recognised the blurred line between contractor and employee status. Scholars such as Todolí-Signes (2021) have long warned that platforms simulate autonomy while maintaining surveillance and control through algorithmic design.

ADGWU is calling on affected members to register interest for case updates via the contact form.
