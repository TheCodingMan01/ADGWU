Union Report Reveals Rise in Algorithmic Wage Theft

Melbourne · September 12, 2034

A bombshell report released today by the Australian Digital Workers Union reveals that thousands of Australian freelancers are being underpaid or retrospectively penalised by platform algorithms in what the union calls “algorithmic wage theft.”

The report — Clocked Out by Code: Algorithmic Wage Theft in Australia’s Platform Economy — found that:

  • 25% of surveyed workers had pay deducted due to algorithmic controls placed on rural workers unable to meet minimum pickup times
  • Many experienced retroactive wage reductions with no appeals process
  • Common targets included platforms like Uber and RideAwAI

According to the report, such systems operate with no transparency, accountability, or human oversight.

Union analyst Sophie Ren called this practice a systemic violation of the right to fair remuneration. She cited Kellerman & Olawunmi (2034), who describe these mechanisms as forms of “automated asymmetry” — where decision-making is entirely algorithmic but consequences are human.

“This isn’t a technical glitch,” Ren said. “It’s the business model.”

The ADGWU will continue to pursue this matter, and will provide regular updates to union members in our weekly newsletter. If you or colleagues have been affected by this, please get in touch via our Contact page.
