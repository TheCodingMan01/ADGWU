First Ethical Digital Labour Platform Launches

Victoria · August 19, 2035

RideMate, a new rideshare app funded by the Australian government and developed in consultation with the ADGWU, has launched with more than 5,000 users in its first week. The platform is being promoted as “putting workers first” and operating in full compliance with the Digital Workers Rights Act 2035.

As RideMate spokesperson Ryan McKenzie explained:

“RideMate is our answer to the long-standing worker exploitation from apps like Uber and Lyft. We understand the injustices that drivers face, and want to create a platform where they feel respected and valued for their time and effort.”

RideMate introduces several worker-first features:

  • Minimum wage guarantees for all jobs, independent of tips
  • A transparent fee calculation system not linked to tip amounts
  • Fuel compensation via IoT devices tracking per-task consumption
  • A detailed dispute system that weighs evidence from both parties

Whether RideMate can maintain its worker-first principles or eventually succumb to platform decay remains to be seen. For now, ADGWU welcomes the launch as a sign that ethical, rights-based alternatives are possible under Australia’s new digital labour laws.
