First Ethical Digital Labour Platform Launches in accordance with the Digital Workers Rights Act

Victoria · August 19, 2035

Victoria, August 19, 2035 - RideMate, a new ride-sharing app, was officially launched last week. Having been developed in consultation with the ADGWU, the platform is being promoted as “putting workers first” through its co-op model, and operating in full compliance with the Digital Workers Rights Act 2035.

Platform capitalist apps such as Uber centralize power to platform owners and shareholders, who govern the conditions of how work and payment are distributed on the app, which workers are subjected to.

In contrast, RideMate uses a platform cooperative model, where the platform is collectively owned by each worker, who has equal rights and say in how it is governed. Instead of trying to maximize profits or platform growth, RideMate provides workers the ability to advocate for sustainable hours and wages. Similar gig working cooperatives, such as Salmo in France and Co-Op Ride in the US already exist, but RideMate is the first of its scale founded in Australia.

In accordance with its co-op principles RideMate boasts the following capabilities:

  • Drivers get the majority of the payment for a job, with 10% commission taken for maintenance and development costs. Tips are also awarded to drivers in full.
  • Drivers are invited to a monthly online meeting with developers where they are able to discuss decisions and potential changes.

Whether RideMate can maintain its worker-first principles or eventually succumb to platform decay remains to be seen. For now, ADGWU welcomes the launch as a sign that ethical, rights-based alternatives are possible under Australia’s new digital labour laws.
