Driverless Cars Disrupt the Rideshare Economy

Melbourne · December 8, 2034

As autonomous vehicles enter the Australian market, analysts warn that the rideshare economy could be reshaped within just two years. Overseas evidence from the United States and Western Europe shows rapid disruption to driver incomes, with many workers displaced as fleets of driverless cars expand.

The ADGWU, in partnership with the Transport Workers Union (TWU), is compiling data on how many Australian drivers may be affected. Early findings include reports of significant earnings loss and reduced bargaining power where automation has already been deployed overseas.

Union leaders argue that governments and platforms must plan now for transition support, fair severance packages, and reskilling pathways. Without intervention, the introduction of autonomous vehicles risks decimating livelihoods in much the same way as seen internationally.
