About Digital Platform Work

The Parliament of Australia defines gig work as “A range of terms describe the gig economy (p. 7): on-demand, gig, sharing (p. 6), collaborative, crowdsourcing, and short-term, project-based and outcome defined independent or ‘freelance’ work.”

A 2019 survey found that 7.1 percent of Australia’s population had offered to work on a digital platform in the past 12 months, but only 0.2 percent were completely reliant on it for income and doing it full time. Thanks to digital platforms increasing the scope and frequency of transactions, reducing the costs of transactions and increasing accessibility and trust in regards to payments from businesses, the Australian gig economy is expanding.

This, coupled with the changing economy causing more workers to seek extra income and workers looking for better work-life balance or more flexible working arrangements, means that the industry is expanding quickly. Distributions of gig work in Australia as of 2019 included concentrations of 18.6 percent in food delivery, 16.9 percent in professional services and 11.6 percent in odd jobs.

In 2021, the First interim report: on-demand platform work in Australia notes that “The terms 'gig economy', 'gig work', 'on-demand work' and 'platform work' are often difficult to define, and their meanings can sometimes be contested.” It references scholars saying that on-demand work is increasing in Australia while legislation isn’t keeping up.

They then go on to clarify that gig work is “closely associated with online platforms” and that while it can resemble labour hire, it has distinct differences in how people are employed (labour hire companies employ and pay people, gig work platforms treat them more like independent contractors) and how reimbursement is then negotiated.

They then define the terms as follows:

  • 'On-demand work' can be a broad term including labour hire, casual work, fixed-term contracts and independent contracting; it can also be used as a more specific term within the gig sector.
  • 'Gig work' can be seen as a subset of on-demand work.
  • 'Platform work' is on-demand work that is facilitated by web-based platforms or apps.
  • Platform work is available across a range of sectors, using different operating models.
  • Most gig or platform workers in Australia are classified as 'independent contractors', rather than 'employees' and are not covered by the Fair Work Act 2009 (Cth).
  • Most gig workers do not have leave entitlements, minimum rates of pay, or other protections of labour regulation.
